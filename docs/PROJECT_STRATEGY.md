# Project Strategy

## Objective

Maximize competition score and placement in the Overcooked-AI project while preserving compatibility with the teacher's `build_policy` workflow.

The immediate priority is to secure strong performance on the three disclosed scenarios. The secondary objective is to prepare a robust generalist for the three undisclosed scenarios.

## Research Basis

The implementation strategy combines two findings:

1. Self-play PPO can learn strong task execution, but policies may overfit to their own coordination conventions and perform poorly with unfamiliar partners.
2. State augmentation can substantially reduce cross-play failures caused by poor state coverage by exposing policies to states generated by different partner behaviors.

Human behavior cloning is useful as one source of partner diversity, but it is not the final target because the competition partners are scripted agents and agents submitted by other groups.

The intended training progression is:

```text
self-play competence
    ↓
state robustness
    ↓
partner-distribution robustness
    ↓
scenario specialization and generalization
```

## Stage A — Self-Play Competence

### Goal

Build a reliable PPO self-play baseline and an end-to-end pipeline from training to evaluation and teacher-compatible inference.

### Expected capabilities

- PPO self-play training
- Reproducible configuration and seeds
- Checkpoint save/load
- Loading through `build_policy`
- Canonical implementation of the official competition score
- Evaluation against both player positions where applicable
- Local smoke tests and Kaggle-compatible training commands

### Exit criteria

- The complete `train → checkpoint → build_policy → evaluation` path works
- Results are reproducible under fixed seeds
- The policy learns non-trivial soup delivery behavior
- The teacher's original workflow remains functional

## Stage B — State Robustness

### Goal

Reduce failures caused by unseen states and unfamiliar execution patterns.

### Expected capabilities

- Multiple independent self-play seeds
- Cross-play evaluation
- Trajectory-state collection
- Configurable state buffer
- Training episodes initialized from sampled valid states
- Comparison between standard and state-augmented self-play

### Dependencies

Stage A must be stable before this begins.

### Exit criteria

- State augmentation is integrated without duplicating environment logic
- Cross-play robustness improves on relevant layouts
- Self-play competence does not regress substantially

## Stage C — Partner-Distribution Robustness

### Goal

Train an ego policy that performs well with a diverse frozen partner pool rather than only with itself.

### Potential partner sources

- Independent self-play policies
- Historical checkpoints
- Behavior-cloned human models
- Teacher-provided scripted partners
- Sticky-action and random-action variants
- Weak or partially trained policies
- Role-specialized policies

### Expected capabilities

- Stable partner interface
- Configurable partner pool
- Partner sampling
- Frozen-partner best-response training
- Evaluation by partner and layout

### Dependencies

Stages A and B should provide reliable training, evaluation, checkpoints, and state coverage.

### Exit criteria

- Robust performance across a representative partner pool
- No dependency on one narrow coordination convention
- No major loss of raw task competence

## Stage D — Competition Specialization

### Goal

Optimize disclosed scenarios directly while maintaining a generalist for undisclosed scenarios.

### Expected capabilities

- Layout-specific configurations or checkpoints
- Exact evaluation against disclosed partners
- Specialist policies for known scenarios
- A generalist policy trained across diverse layouts and partners
- Checkpoint selection using the official score and robustness metrics

### Dependencies

The previous stages must provide stable components that can be reused without major redesign.

### Exit criteria

- Strong official-score performance on scenarios 1–3
- Robust fallback behavior on unfamiliar layouts and partners
- Deployment remains compatible with `build_policy`

## Evaluation Principles

Checkpoint selection must prioritize:

1. Number of soups
2. Consistency across seeds
3. Official competition score
4. Low probability of zero-soup episodes
5. Low timeout and deadlock rates

Shaped training reward must not be used as the sole model-selection criterion.

## Scope Boundaries

Do not implement all stages at once.

Each stage should be:

1. Planned using the current repository state
2. Implemented as a complete vertical slice
3. Validated
4. Documented
5. Stabilized before the next stage begins

The following are out of scope until simpler methods are saturated:

- Coupled planning and replanning
- Complex Theory-of-Mind systems
- Large transformer-based partner models
- Advanced population methods that add significant complexity without proven need
